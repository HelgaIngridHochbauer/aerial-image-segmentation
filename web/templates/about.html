Mission

About URBAN-SYNAPSE

The URBAN-SYNAPSE project focuses on the automated detection of vehicles, buildings, and roads through high-density aerial imagery. As drone technology advances, there is an increasing demand for systems that can provide real-time telemetry and precise mapping of complex urban environments.

Our Models & Datasets

YOLOv8 OBB

Used for precise Oriented Bounding Box detection of vehicles. Trained on the VSAI dataset, allowing for rotation-aware vehicle tracking in highly congested aerial environments.

U-Net Segmentation

A classic semantic segmentation architecture trained on the ISPRS Potsdam dataset. It maps the terrain into 6 main classes: roads/pavement, buildings, low vegetation, trees, cars, and clutter.

Advanced Morphological Post-Processing

To provide production-ready segmentation masks, our pipeline applies intelligent morphological operations directly to the U-Net inference outputs:

  • Class-Specific Structuring: Different classes receive tailored kernels. For example, buildings are cleaned with 7x7 rectangular kernels, while vegetation uses 5x5 elliptical kernels to preserve natural, organic shapes.
  • Shape-Based Geometric Filtering: We analyze the boundaries of every detected building. If a "building" is extremely elongated (aspect ratio > 7.0) or non-compact (circularity < 0.07), it is automatically corrected and reclassified as a road.
  • Cross-Class Overlap Heuristic: We leverage data from multiple classes. If a car (Class 4) is detected completely inside or on top of a building footprint, our system instantly flags the topological impossibility and reverts the underlying structure to a road.
